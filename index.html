Context

"Walkability" refers to how easily people can walk in a given area, like a neighborhood or city. It includes factors like the proximity of destinations, the presence of sidewalks, pedestrian safety, and other elements that either facilitate or hinder walking as a mode of transportation

-> A map allows for a quick and intuitive visual representation of areas with high or low walkability. This offers an immediate visual understanding of pedestrian friendliness in a given region.

-> Walkability index maps highlight specific features contributing to pedestrian friendliness, such as population density, diversity, connectivity, proximity, etc. This helps identify strengths and weaknesses in different areas. .

-> Local authorities, urban planners, and policymakers can use these maps to make informed decisions on urban planning. They can pinpoint areas that require pedestrian improvements and design policies to promote walkability.

Connectivity index

Seamless connections between destinations, reducing the need for car travel.

Formulate:

Connectivity = number of true intersections / km2 of land area

Density index

Density measures the level of activity in an area-be it in terms of population, housing, or employment. It reflects compact land development and its correlation with reduced reliance on motorized transport in urban settings.

Formula:

Household Density = Number of households / km2 of residential land
